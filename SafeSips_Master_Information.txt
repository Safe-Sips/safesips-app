01 Executive snapshot

The complete project in one page.

One-sentence definition
SafeSips is a discreet, pen-like drink-screening concept that uses three microfluidic reaction channels, an optical readout, a soft LED signal, and a privacy-first mobile app to provide a preliminary warning and connected support during nights out.

Core promise: Keep safety close without turning a night out into a warning scene.
Tagline: Your best party friend.
Closing line: Safety shouldn’t be a privilege — it should be something you can have in your pocket.
Primary audience: Young women aged 18–30, students, and party-goers; secondary audience includes all event-goers aged 18–35 and B2B safety partners.
Final product look: A slim, normal matte-black pen with a subtle dark clip, a sampling tip, a small transparent microfluidic window, and a thin hot-pink LED ring.
Targets screened in concept: GHB, ketamine, and Rohypnol through separate channels.
App functions: Instant Bluetooth alert, trusted-contact notification, optional location sharing, anonymous community map, and privacy controls.
App implementation status (June 2026): Anonymous community map with masked real-time location sharing is live on web and mobile. Security hardening, draft legal UI, and production env configuration added June 13, 2026. Bluetooth alerts and trusted-contact notification are not yet built.
Commercial model: Reusable electronic device plus sealed, single-use test cartridges; direct-to-consumer, refills, venue packs, and optional B2B service.
Brand feeling: Warm, empowering, stylish, calm, credible, youthful, and non-clinical.

What makes SafeSips different
- It integrates chemistry, automatic optical reading, a discreet object, and a connected response system rather than offering only a disposable visual strip.
- It makes safety feel like a normal part of a night out, not a fear-based ritual.
- It connects private individual support with wider anonymous community awareness.
- It treats validation and transparent limitations as part of the product, not only as legal fine print.
02 Strategic direction

The north star that should guide product, design, and communication decisions.

Creative north star
The pen that becomes a guardian. A normal object quietly reveals an invisible microfluidic world, turns a chemical signal into a calm alert, and extends into a trusted support circle.

Desired user reaction
The project should leave a visitor thinking: “I would actually carry this. It feels useful, calm, discreet, and designed for real life.”
Strategic principles

Principle: Meaning
Discretion before drama: The product should fit naturally into a bag, pocket, hand, club, festival, campus, or trip.
Empowerment before fear: Show confidence, preparation, support, and agency. Never build the brand around victim imagery or panic.
Science made understandable: Translate microfluidics and colorimetry into a four-step story: sample, separate, react, alert.
Trust before speed: No launch deadline or marketing claim should outrank performance, independent validation, or clarity about limitations.
Connection without surveillance: The app should support trusted contacts and anonymous awareness while minimizing personal and location data.
A product ecosystem, not a gadget: The pen, cartridge, app, trusted circle, venue protocol, and community map must feel like one coherent system.

Emotional journey
Beauty → curiosity → action → intelligence → connection → safety → empowerment.
An earlier version also expressed this as sparkle → curiosity → trust → protection → connection → empowerment. Both describe the same progression: attract first, explain second, reassure last.
Non-negotiable exclusions
- No hospital, medical-device, crime-scene, dark-alley, or victim aesthetic.
- No red siren screens, aggressive alarms, or fear-heavy copy.
- No implication that the user is responsible for preventing assault.
- No “100% accurate,” “guaranteed safe,” “detects everything,” “medical grade,” or “forensic proof” claims.
- No product styling that makes the pen resemble a vape, lipstick, or science-fiction gadget.

04 Product definition

What the physical product is, how it should look, and how it should be used.

Final product direction — confirmed
A normal matte-black pen, not a pearlescent lavender beauty object. It should look discreet and believable before it looks technological.

Physical architecture

Element: Direction
Body: Slim cylindrical body, clean matte-black finish, restrained premium proportions.
Clip: Subtle dark clip; useful and unobtrusive.
Sampling end: Slightly tapered tip or intake end, shown entering the drink tip-first.
Microfluidic window: Small transparent area that reveals the three channels without making the whole pen look like lab equipment.
Indicator: Thin hot-pink LED ring or soft light indicator; calm pulse, not an alarm flash.
Electronics: Optical reader, Bluetooth Low Energy, battery, USB-C charging in the commercial concept.
Consumable: Sealed, single-use cartridge to reduce contamination and support consistent testing.

Intended user flow
1. Carry the reusable pen body and sealed cartridge.
2. Insert or activate a fresh cartridge.
3. Place the sampling tip into the drink or collect a tiny sample.
4. Allow the sample to move through three separate channels.
5. The device reads the color response with an optical sensor.
6. A soft LED and app notification indicate a possible suspicious signal.
7. The app presents calm next actions and can contact trusted people with consent.
8. Dispose of the used cartridge and retain the reusable electronic body.
Experience requirements
- The check must be discreet enough to use without creating a scene.
- The physical interaction must be understandable without long instructions.
- The user should never need to interpret subtle colors under club lighting; the sensor assists the reading.
- The device must communicate uncertainty clearly and never label a negative result as proof of safety.
- The product should support the whole group and trusted circle, not only one isolated user.
Commercial product family — proposed

Offer | Contents | Role
Starter Kit | Reusable pen, 3 cartridges, USB-C cable, app access. | Initial acquisition and installed user base.
Refill 3 | 3 sealed cartridges with lot code and expiry. | Low-commitment occasional use.
Refill 10 | 10 sealed cartridges. | Recurring use and lower cost per test.
Venue Pack | 5 devices, 100 cartridges, display stand, staff training. | B2B deployment and visible venue responsibility.
Venue Care | Support, education materials, device administration, aggregated statistics. | Optional recurring B2B service.

05 Scientific and technical concept

The proposed mechanism, presented accurately and without overclaiming.

Scientific status — validate
The reaction choices, limits of detection, speed, selectivity, shelf life, interference from real drinks, and false-positive/false-negative rates are research hypotheses until independently tested.

Simplified mechanism
Drink sample → capillary intake → three microchannels → dried reagent chemistry → optical/color response → sensor interpretation → LED alert → Bluetooth app notification.

Step | Public explanation | Technical concept
Sample | A tiny amount of drink enters the pen tip. | Capillary or micro-sampling intake.
Separate | The sample moves into three tiny channels. | Multi-channel microfluidic cartridge.
React | Built-in test chemistry creates a color signal. | Dried or immobilized reagents react with target compounds.
Read | The pen checks the signal. | Optical sensor or reflectance/color measurement.
Alert | The pen lights up and informs the phone. | LED plus Bluetooth Low Energy event.

Three-channel research concept

Channel | Target | Visual language | Candidate chemistry from earlier briefs
1 | GHB | Warm gold/yellow glow. | NAD+-linked oxidation concept; requires full laboratory validation.
2 | Ketamine | Electric blue glow. | Simon’s-reagent / secondary-amine color response concept; requires validation in drinks.
3 | Rohypnol | Purple-red or magenta glow. | Zimmermann-type benzodiazepine response concept; requires validation and selectivity testing.

Required validation programme
- Test each candidate reaction across a wide panel of alcoholic and non-alcoholic drinks, colors, acidity levels, sugars, carbonation, and common mixers.
- Measure sensitivity, specificity, response time, detection limits, repeatability, and stability across temperature and storage conditions.
- Quantify false positives and false negatives; design controls and lot-release criteria.
- Validate optical reading under realistic lighting and with varying sample colors.
- Verify cartridge sealing, contamination control, reagent shelf life, and safe disposal.
- Use an independent laboratory before public performance claims or a commercial pilot.
Approved accuracy wording
Use: “preliminary screening concept,” “possible suspicious signal,” “modelled prototype target,” or “research estimate.”
Avoid: “safe drink,” “guaranteed detection,” “100% accurate,” “medical grade,” “forensic evidence,” or any claim that the device replaces professional or laboratory testing.

App visual direction
- Floating phone, not a standard two-column feature layout.
- Dark navy glass UI, lavender background, hot-pink highlights, soft cards, and calm animations.
- Four feature cards can orbit the phone like protective petals.
- Map pins should look like anonymous pink or gold blooms, not crime markers.

- Collect only the data needed for the user-requested function.

- Separate private trusted-contact alerts from public anonymous reports.

B. Implemented live safety map — shipped June 2026

Status: IMPLEMENTED prototype at safesips-app/ (web + server + shared + mobile). This is the current public-facing web experience: a single-page real-time privacy map, not the nine-chapter marketing scroll site above.

What the live map does
- Lets any visitor share an approximate location on a live map while keeping their exact GPS position private.
- Shows the user’s exact position as a blue dot on their device only; all other users see a yellow 200 m privacy circle centered on a randomized point within 50 m of the true location.
- Broadcasts masked locations in real time to every connected user via WebSockets (Socket.io).
- Supports anonymous multi-user presence: each session gets a server-assigned UUID; no accounts, profiles, or display names.

User-facing features (web)
- Share My Location — requests geolocation permission only when pressed; high-accuracy GPS with 10 s timeout.
- Enter your address + Go — geocodes free text via OpenStreetMap Nominatim for users without GPS or on desktop.
- Address autocomplete — as the user types (≥3 characters), Nominatim suggestions appear above the field; selecting one shares immediately using the resolved coordinates. Labels show place name, street, and city only.
- Update — re-runs the last source (GPS or address) and generates a fresh random mask each time.
- Stop sharing — removes the public circle from all clients immediately.
- Sensitive-location warning modal — shown before the first share; warns against sharing from home, school, workplace, shelter, or medical facility; includes “not 911” emergency disclaimer; acknowledgment persisted in localStorage.
- Privacy Policy & Terms — footer links open in-app legal modals (draft summaries; require lawyer review before launch).
- Live control panel — connection status (Online / Connecting / Offline), sharing status with pulse indicator, last-update timer, count of others nearby, privacy note, and map legend.
- Full-screen interactive map — Leaflet + OpenStreetMap tiles; auto-recenter on share; dark-themed UI with glassmorphism panel.
- Responsive layout — bottom-left glass control panel on desktop; full-width bottom sheet on screens ≤560 px with scroll.
- PWA manifest — installable metadata (standalone, theme colors, icon); no service worker yet.
- Accessibility — modal dialog roles, form labels, prefers-reduced-motion disables circle animations.

============================================================
SAFESIPS APP: LOCATION SHARING, PRIVACY, AND REAL-TIME MAP
(Original specification — see section 19 for implemented status)
============================================================

APP PURPOSE

The SafeSips app should include a real-time web and mobile map that allows users to share an approximate location while preserving their exact position. The map is intended to support safety alerts, community visibility, and live presence without publicly exposing a user's precise GPS coordinates.

CORE USER FLOW

1. The user opens the app.
2. The user either presses "Share My Location" or enters an address manually.
3. The app obtains or geocodes the exact location.
4. The user's exact position appears locally as a blue dot and is visible only to that user.
5. The app generates a randomized public location within 50 meters of the exact location.
6. A yellow, semi-transparent privacy circle with a 200-meter radius is centered on the randomized point.
7. The circle is broadcast in real time to all active users.
8. Connected users receive location updates instantly without refreshing the app.
9. Inactive users are removed after a configurable timeout.

1. LOCATION DETECTION BUTTON

The main location action should be a prominent button labeled:

"Share My Location"

When pressed, the app should:

- Request geolocation permission only at that moment.
- Retrieve the user's current GPS coordinates.
- Center the map on the user's position.
- Display the exact position as a blue dot.
- Keep the exact position visible only on the user's own device.
- Generate a separate privacy-preserving public location.

The interface should clearly explain why location access is being requested and what will and will not be shared.

2. MANUAL ADDRESS INPUT

The app should also provide a text input with the placeholder:

"Enter your address"

When an address is submitted, the app should:

- Geocode the address into latitude and longitude coordinates.
- Center the map on the resulting location.
- Display the exact location as a private blue dot.
- Generate the privacy-preserving public location using the same masking method as GPS-based sharing.

Manual input is important for users who deny GPS permission, use a desktop device, or want to share a different relevant location.

3. PRIVACY-PRESERVING LOCATION MASKING

The exact location must never be publicly transmitted or displayed.

For each update:

- Generate a random point no more than 50 meters from the user's true location.
- Use a geographically correct random offset rather than adding arbitrary latitude and longitude values.
- Use the randomized point only as the center of the public privacy area.
- Recalculate the random offset every time the user updates their location.
- Do not send the user's exact coordinates to other clients.
- Avoid storing exact coordinates on the backend whenever possible.

The random point should be selected uniformly by area inside the 50-meter radius. This avoids concentrating generated locations near the center.

4. PUBLIC VISIBILITY AREA

The publicly visible representation should be a circular area with:

- Radius: exactly 200 meters.
- Center: the randomized location, never the exact location.
- Fill color: yellow.
- Fill style: semi-transparent.
- Border: animated pulsing or blinking.
- Animation style: similar to a radar ping or scanning effect.

The public circle communicates that the user is somewhere within a broad area. It must not imply that the center point is the user's real position.

The interface should include a brief explanation such as:

"Your precise location stays private. Others see only an approximate 200 m area."

5. MAP DISPLAY

The map should:

- Show the user's exact location as a blue dot only on that user's device.
- Show the user's yellow animated privacy circle.
- Show the active privacy circles of other users.
- Support zooming and panning.
- Automatically center on the user's location after a new update.
- Work responsively on desktop and mobile.
- Avoid exposing exact coordinates through labels, popups, browser logs, URLs, analytics, or client-side debugging output.

Suitable map technologies include:

- Mapbox GL JS
- Leaflet
- OpenStreetMap tiles

The final technology should be selected based on licensing, expected traffic, mobile performance, customization needs, and operating cost.

6. REAL-TIME SYNCHRONIZATION

The app must synchronize active public locations instantly.

Required behavior:

- When a user updates their location, all connected clients receive the new randomized center and privacy circle.
- Updates appear without refreshing the page.
- A newly connected user immediately receives all currently active public locations.
- Disconnections and stale sessions are handled automatically.
- Updates should be rate-limited to prevent abuse and unnecessary network traffic.

Possible technologies include:

- WebSockets
- Socket.io
- Firebase Realtime Database
- Supabase Realtime

Only the randomized public coordinates, public status, user identifier, and necessary timestamps should be synchronized.

7. MULTI-USER PRESENCE

The system should support multiple simultaneous users.

For each active user, the app should maintain:

- A unique, non-identifying user or session ID.
- The current randomized public center point.
- The time of the latest update.
- An active or inactive status.
- An expiration time or presence timeout.

Every connected client should display the user's public privacy circle.

The system should:

- Update circles in real time.
- Remove circles after a configurable inactivity timeout.
- Allow temporary or anonymous sessions where appropriate.
- Avoid displaying personal names unless the user explicitly chooses to share one.
- Prevent one user from reading another user's private data.

8. SECURITY AND PRIVACY REQUIREMENTS

The application must:

- Never transmit exact coordinates to other users.
- Preferably never send exact coordinates to the server.
- Transmit only the randomized public center.
- Use HTTPS for all web traffic.
- Use WSS for WebSocket traffic.
- Request geolocation permission only when necessary.
- Minimize or eliminate stored location history.
- Use strict backend access controls.
- Validate all incoming coordinates and payloads.
- Rate-limit location updates.
- Protect against spoofing, scraping, enumeration, and automated tracking.
- Avoid exposing sensitive data in analytics and error-reporting tools.
- Remove expired location records.
- Provide a clear option to stop sharing immediately.
- Provide a visible sharing-status indicator.

The app should not claim that the system guarantees anonymity. It should state clearly that location masking reduces precision but does not eliminate every privacy risk.

9. SAFE SHARING CONTROLS

The app should include:

- Start sharing.
- Stop sharing.
- Update location.
- Sharing status.
- Time since last update.
- Optional automatic expiration.
- Optional emergency removal of the user's public circle.
- A warning before the user shares from sensitive places such as a home address, school, workplace, shelter, or medical facility.

The user should always know whether a location is currently being shared.

10. PERFORMANCE AND SCALABILITY

The production version should:

- Be optimized for hundreds of concurrent users.
- Use efficient real-time subscriptions.
- Avoid rebroadcasting unchanged data.
- Cluster or simplify map rendering when many circles are visible.
- Use server-side validation and cleanup.
- Handle reconnects and temporary network loss.
- Work on common modern mobile and desktop browsers.
- Degrade gracefully when geolocation, animations, or real-time connectivity are unavailable.

11. RECOMMENDED DATA MODEL

A public presence record may include:

- public_user_id
- randomized_latitude
- randomized_longitude
- privacy_radius_meters, fixed at 200
- updated_at
- expires_at
- sharing_status

The record must not include the exact latitude or longitude.

Exact coordinates should remain temporarily in local device memory only for the purpose of generating the public randomized point and positioning the private blue dot.

12. IMPORTANT IMPLEMENTATION DETAIL

The randomized offset must be calculated on the user's device before any public update is sent. The backend should receive only the masked point.

A suitable approach is:

- Select a random angle from 0 to 2π.
- Select a random distance using the square root of a uniformly generated value, multiplied by 50 meters.
- Convert that distance and direction into a geographic offset.
- Use the result as the public center.
- Discard any exact-coordinate payload before network transmission.

This method produces a random point distributed evenly by area within the 50-meter masking circle.

13. APP DESIGN DIRECTION

The app interface should match the wider SafeSips visual identity:

- Matte-black or very dark base.
- High-contrast yellow for public privacy zones and alerts.
- Blue reserved for the user's private exact-location dot.
- Radar-like pulses and scanning animations.
- Minimal, calm, safety-focused interface.
- Clear state changes rather than decorative complexity.
- Strong mobile usability and large touch targets.
- Direct language with no unnecessary technical jargon.

The map should feel like a live safety layer, not a social tracking tool.

14. VALIDATION AND OPEN QUESTIONS

Before launch, SafeSips should determine:

- Whether the 50-meter random offset and 200-meter public radius provide sufficient privacy in low-density areas.
- Whether circles should be visible globally, locally, to trusted groups, or only during active incidents.
- How long a public circle should remain active.
- Whether users can choose a shorter sharing duration.
- Whether public circles are anonymous or linked to verified SafeSips accounts.
- How location spoofing will be detected or communicated.
- Whether exact local coordinates are ever needed for emergency-service integration.
- What legal basis and consent flow apply to location processing.
- What retention and deletion rules apply in each launch market.

These settings should be validated through privacy review, security testing, user research, and legal review before production release.

SAFESIPS APP v2.0: ACCOUNT-BASED SAFETY PLATFORM (IMPLEMENTED)
Community accounts, reports, safe havens, first-aid, check-ins, forum
Implemented July 2026 — supersedes the original map-only specification above
============================================================

A0. WHAT CHANGED (SUMMARY)

The original app (v1.x) was a stateless, anonymous, in-memory real-time
privacy map: no accounts, no database, one anonymous circle per connection.
v2.0 rebuilds it into a full account-based safety platform while KEEPING the
core privacy guarantee for live presence. Everything below is implemented and
verified end-to-end (build + unit tests + browser run).

New capabilities added in v2.0:
- Personal accounts with anti-bot protection (email verification, rate
  limiting, and a pluggable Cloudflare Turnstile hook).
- A profile with a Reddit-style activity history and engagement badges
  (bronze / silver / gold = level 1 / 2 / 3).
- Location safety reports that other users can upvote (one vote per user).
- Safe-haven suggestions: marking a place unsafe (or tapping "Help near me")
  surfaces nearby police, hospital, fire station, fuel, pharmacy, and 24/7
  locations on the map.
- Spiking / drink-contamination first-aid guidance (first effects + what to
  do first + how to help someone else).
- An hourly check-in "dead-man's-switch" with a personal security question;
  a missed check-in escalates to the user's primary SOS contact.
- A waitlist / newsletter signup.
- A community safety forum (threads + replies with up/down voting).
- Real verification emails via SMTP (nodemailer).

Locked product decisions behind this rebuild:
- Persistence: SQLite (better-sqlite3), file-based, no external database.
- Accounts are REQUIRED for the whole app; the live map is behind login.
- The on-the-wire location privacy invariant is preserved (exact presence
  coordinates never leave the device; the broadcast id is never tied to an
  account).
- Anti-bot: built-in (email verification + throttling) now, with a pluggable
  Cloudflare Turnstile that activates only when its secret is configured.
- Platform scope: web + server + shared. The Expo mobile app was NOT updated
  in this pass (still the v1.x map; see A19).

A1. ARCHITECTURE & TECH STACK

Monorepo (npm workspaces): shared / server / web (+ separate Expo mobile,
out of scope for v2.0).
- shared (@safesips/shared): wire types, DTOs, location masking, engagement
  badge logic. Single source of truth for web + server.
- server (@safesips/server): Node ESM, Express + Socket.io, SQLite. New deps:
  better-sqlite3 (embedded DB), bcryptjs (hashing), jsonwebtoken (JWT),
  express-rate-limit (HTTP limits), zod (request validation), nodemailer
  (email). TypeScript 5.6, tsx for dev.
- web (@safesips/web): React 18 + Vite + Leaflet/react-leaflet + socket.io.
  New dep: react-router-dom v7 (client routing).

A2. ACCOUNTS & AUTHENTICATION

- Register (POST /api/auth/register): email + password (min 8) + display name
  (2-40). Optional captcha token. Creates an unverified account, hashes the
  password (bcrypt), issues a session token, and sends a verification email.
  Duplicate email -> 409.
- Login (POST /api/auth/login): returns a token + user. Unknown emails are
  compared against a dummy hash to equalize timing (reduces user enumeration).
- Verify (POST /api/auth/verify): consumes a single-use email token.
- me / logout / resend-verification: GET /api/auth/me, POST /api/auth/logout
  (revokes the session), POST /api/auth/resend-verification.
- Password hashing: bcryptjs, cost factor BCRYPT_ROUNDS (default 11). The same
  hashing is reused for check-in answers.
- Tokens: JWT (jsonwebtoken) carrying { sub: userId, jti: sessionId, ev:
  emailVerified }, valid JWT_TTL_DAYS (default 7). Every token is backed by a
  revocable row in the "sessions" table, so logout kills the token
  server-side immediately. The token is sent as an Authorization: Bearer
  header for REST and in the Socket.io handshake auth for the websocket.
- Email-verify gate: users can log in immediately, but posting, reporting, and
  starting a check-in plan require a verified email (voting does NOT).

A3. ANTI-BOT / "ENSURE REAL PEOPLE USE THE APP"

Layered defenses so bots and spammers cannot flood the app:
1. Email verification is required before a user can create public content.
2. Auth throttling: a sliding window (auth_attempts table) locks out after
   AUTH_MAX_FAILS (6) failures per 15 minutes, keyed per email AND per
   hashed IP; plus a coarse express-rate-limit across all /api/auth routes.
3. Pluggable Cloudflare Turnstile (privacy-friendly captcha): when
   TURNSTILE_SECRET is set, register + waitlist verify the token server-side;
   when unset, captcha is skipped and the built-in defenses apply. A
   client-claimed "captcha passed" flag is never trusted.
4. Waitlist is heavily throttled (20 / hour / IP) and deduplicated by email.

A4. PROFILE: HISTORY & ENGAGEMENT BADGES

- Reddit-style activity history: an activity_log records report_created,
  report_upvoted, thread_created, post_created, and checkin_completed. The
  profile shows a chronological feed plus counts.
- Engagement score = weighted sum of contributions: reports x5, upvotes given
  x1, upvotes received x3, threads x5, posts x2, check-ins completed x2.
- Badge tiers (thresholds by score): none (Newcomer), bronze (>=10, Level 1),
  silver (>=50, Level 2), gold (>=200, Level 3).
- Badges are a PURE FUNCTION of derived counts — no badge state is stored, so
  the web and server always agree and never drift. Endpoints:
  GET /api/users/me/stats, GET /api/users/me/activity.

A5. SAFETY REPORTS & COMMUNITY UPVOTING

- Users mark a place safe or unsafe with an optional category tag and note.
  Reports are stored in the "reports" table and shown on the map as colored
  markers (unsafe red, safe green).
- IMPORTANT — two distinct location privacy contracts:
  (a) Presence circle = passive, masked (~50 m offset), anonymous.
  (b) Safety report = an INTENTIONAL, user-confirmed publish of the EXACT
      point, attributed to the author's display name. The report dialog shows
      an explicit warning distinct from the presence warning ("This publishes
      the exact location ... only share places, never your home").
- Upvoting: any logged-in user can upvote a report; ONE vote per user
  (enforced by a composite primary key report_votes(report_id, user_id)); you
  cannot upvote your own report. New/removed reports broadcast in real time
  (socket report:new / report:removed).
- Endpoints: GET /api/reports?bbox=..., POST /api/reports (verified),
  POST|DELETE /api/reports/:id/vote, DELETE /api/reports/:id (author only).

A6. SAFE HAVENS ("offer safe havens as surrounding locations")

- When a user marks a spot UNSAFE, the app immediately surfaces nearby help;
  a standalone "Help near me" button does the same anytime (using current
  location, falling back to the map center).
- Server proxies the OpenStreetMap Overpass API
  (GET /api/safe-havens?lat&lng&radius) for: police, hospital, fire_station,
  fuel, pharmacy, and any 24/7-open place. Results are de-duplicated, sorted
  by distance (haversine), capped at 30, and cached in-memory (5-min TTL).
- The panel lists each place with distance, "open 24/7" flag, a "Call" link
  (tel:), and a "Directions" link (OpenStreetMap); haven pins also render on
  the map. If Overpass is unavailable, it degrades gracefully to a "call 112"
  message and never blocks the unsafe-report flow.

A7. CONTAMINATION / SPIKING FIRST-AID

Static, emergency-forward safety content (web/src/content/firstAid.ts):
- First effects to watch for (10), including "losing strength in your legs —
  they feel heavy, weak or won't hold you", trouble moving/controlling your
  body, sudden dizziness, confusion, strong drowsiness, memory gaps.
- What to do FIRST (ordered): (1) tell someone you trust right now; (2) get to
  a safe, well-lit, public place; (3) call 112 if you feel very unwell; (4)
  stop drinking / protect your drink; (5) arrange safe transport with a
  trusted person; (6) note what happened.
- How to help someone else (stay with them; recovery position + call 112 if
  drowsy/unconscious; tell venue security, keep the drink for testing).
- Prominent "Call 112" call-to-action and a clear "not medical advice"
  disclaimer. The page is reachable publicly at /help (logged out) and in-app
  at /first-aid. NOTE: this copy must be reviewed by a qualified person before
  public launch.

A8. CHECK-IN SYSTEM + SOS ESCALATION ("dead-man's switch")

For going somewhere unfamiliar:
- The user creates a plan: interval (default hourly), grace window, a personal
  security question, and the expected answer (hashed, never stored in plain
  text). A primary SOS contact is required before a plan can arm.
- On schedule, the app prompts the user with the question; they must be
  clear-headed enough to type the correct answer within the grace window.
- A SERVER-SIDE scheduler (sweeps every ~15 s) drives the occurrence state
  machine, so escalation happens even if the app is closed:
    pending -> prompted (push checkin:due to the user) -> answered (correct)
    prompted -> missed -> escalated (deadline passed with no correct answer)
  A correct answer schedules the next occurrence; pausing/ending a plan
  cancels pending occurrences.
- On escalation, the primary SOS contact is notified to check on the user and
  the event is recorded in checkin_escalations.
- Endpoints: GET/POST /api/checkins/plans, POST /api/checkins/plans/:id/pause
  and /end, GET /api/checkins/active, POST
  /api/checkins/occurrences/:id/answer, GET /api/checkins/occurrences.
  SOS contacts: GET/POST /api/sos-contacts, DELETE /api/sos-contacts/:id.
- STATUS: the notifier is currently a stub — it records the escalation and
  logs it; wiring it to real SMS/email is the remaining step (the SMTP seam
  already exists; see A10 and A19).

A9. WAITLIST / NEWSLETTER

- Public signup (POST /api/waitlist): name, email, optional country, optional
  interest (user / venue / volunteer / press / investor). Deduplicated by
  email, throttled per IP (hashed), optional captcha. Always returns success
  (never reveals whether the email already existed).

A10. COMMUNITY SAFETY FORUM

- Threads + replies focused on keeping the community safe. Creating a thread
  also creates its first post (atomic). Replies bump the thread's activity.
- Posts support UP/DOWN voting with a net score (upvotes minus downvotes) and
  per-viewer vote state; one vote per user per post; you cannot vote on your
  own post. (Location reports, by contrast, are upvote-only.)
- Endpoints: GET /api/forum/threads (cursor paginated), POST
  /api/forum/threads (verified), GET /api/forum/threads/:id, POST
  /api/forum/threads/:id/posts (verified), POST|DELETE
  /api/forum/posts/:id/vote.

A11. REAL-TIME LAYER & THE PRIVACY INVARIANT

- The Socket.io connection is now AUTHENTICATED: a valid JWT is required in
  the handshake or the connection is rejected. The server knows which account
  owns a socket (needed to push check-in prompts), but:
  - Presence is still masked: the client offsets its exact location by up to
    50 m and only the masked 200 m center is broadcast.
  - The broadcast publicId is a fresh random per-connection UUID, NEVER
    derived from the account — other clients can never tell who is where.
  - Exact coordinates never touch the database (in-memory presence only);
    only opt-in reports persist an exact point.
- Realtime events: presence:* (unchanged), plus report:new / report:removed
  (to all clients) and checkin:due (only to the owning user's sockets).

A12. EMAIL DELIVERY

- nodemailer SMTP transport (auth/email.ts). When SMTP_* is configured, real
  verification emails are sent (HTML + text, "Verify my email" button). When
  unconfigured, the link is logged in development so testing works without a
  mailbox. Sending never throws — a mail failure will not break registration
  (the user can resend). Verified working via Gmail SMTP (app password).

A13. DATA MODEL (SQLite)

Opened with PRAGMA journal_mode=WAL, foreign_keys=ON, busy_timeout=5000.
All primary keys are TEXT UUIDs; timestamps are integer epoch-ms; schema is
created idempotently and tracked by a schema_version row. Tables:
- users, email_verifications, sessions, auth_attempts
- reports, report_votes
- forum_threads, forum_posts, forum_post_votes
- activity_log
- sos_contacts, checkin_plans, checkin_occurrences, checkin_escalations
- waitlist
Only "reports" store exact coordinates (by intent); presence is never
persisted. IPs and email/verify tokens are stored only as salted/sha256
hashes, never raw.

A14. REST API SURFACE (all under /api)

- auth: register, login, verify, me, logout, resend-verification
  (+ test-token in dev/CI only).
- reports: list (bbox), create, vote, unvote, delete.
- safe-havens: nearby help lookup (Overpass proxy).
- forum: threads (list/create/detail), posts (create), post vote/unvote.
- users: me/stats (score + badge), me/activity (history feed).
- sos-contacts: list, create, delete.
- checkins: plans (list/create/pause/end), active, occurrence answer,
  occurrences history.
- waitlist: public signup.
Auth levels: most routes require an account; content creation (reports,
threads, posts, check-in plans) also requires a verified email; waitlist is
public. Request bodies are validated with zod; JSON body limit 32 kb.

A15. WEB APP STRUCTURE

- Routing (react-router-dom): public routes /login, /register, /verify,
  /waitlist, /help (public first-aid). Everything else is behind
  RequireAuth + an AppShell: / (map), /forum, /forum/new, /forum/:id,
  /checkins, /first-aid, /profile.
- Auth is held in an AuthProvider (token in localStorage key "safesips.auth";
  a 401 anywhere logs the user out; the session rehydrates on boot).
- A single authenticated Socket provider owns the websocket for presence,
  reports, and check-ins.
- Pages: Login, Register, VerifyEmail, Waitlist, Map, ForumList, NewThread,
  ForumThread, Profile, CheckinSetup, FirstAid.
- Key components: AppShell (+ unverified-email banner + global check-in
  modal), NavBar, CaptchaWidget, MarkUnsafeDialog, ReportPanel,
  SafeHavenPanel, BadgeChip, PostItem, ActiveCheckinModal, plus the original
  Controls / MapView / PrivacyCircle / AddressInput / SensitiveWarning /
  LegalFooter / LegalModal.
- Navigation: tabs are Map, Forum, Check-in, First aid. The user's display
  name (top-right) links to the profile — there is no separate Profile tab.
- Map behavior: centers on the user on entry (view-only), a "+ Report a
  place" flow (tap the map or use current location) and a "Help near me"
  button; safe-haven pins render on the map; reports update live.
